# What Does ARI Do? Real-World Scenarios

Let's answer the big question with practical examples. Here's what ARI does in real situations.

## Scenario 1: Conference & Trade Show Booth 🎤

**The Setup**: Company booth at a tech conference with 500+ attendees

**What ARI Does**:
- Greets visitors as they approach: "Hi! Welcome to our booth!"
- Drives around the booth area, drawing attention
- Answers questions: "What do you do?" → ARI explains the company
- Captures photos with attendees (Selfie-Cam feature)
- Displays company videos on its screen
- Collects feedback: "What did you think?" → displays form on screen

**Result**: Memorable interaction, media coverage, engagement metrics

---

## Scenario 2: Museum Guided Tour 🏛️

**The Setup**: History museum with exhibits across multiple rooms

**What ARI Does**:
- Guides visitors room-to-room
- Stops at each exhibit and explains it in natural language
- Shows relevant images/videos on its screen
- Answers visitor questions
- Navigates autonomously around obstacles and other visitors
- Plays audio with high-quality speakers

**Result**: Engaging self-guided tour, reduced need for human staff

---

## Scenario 3: Educational Classroom Lesson 🎓

**The Setup**: University class on AI and robotics (20 students)

**What ARI Does**:
- Demonstrates AI concepts live during lecture
- Students control ARI from their phones to show understanding
- Displays real-time sensor data on screen
- Responds to voice commands: "Move forward 2 meters"
- Captures video of its movement for analysis
- Hosts security learning game (Gandalf feature)

**Result**: Hands-on learning, better understanding of concepts, student engagement

## What Makes These Work?

### The "Wow Factor" 
ARI isn't just an intercom or display—it's **present, interactive, and aware**:
- Sees you (via cameras)
- Listens to you (via microphone)
- Responds to you (via voice)
- Moves toward/away from you (via motion)

This creates **engagement** that static signs and speakers don't.

### The Flexibility
Same robot, different applications:
- With phone control = manual demo
- With autonomous navigation = self-guided tour
- With voice commands = hands-free operation
- With photos = event documentation
